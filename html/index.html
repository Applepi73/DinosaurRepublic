Dinosaurs - what are they?

Dinosaurs were spectacular reptiles which ruled Earth for more than 150 million years. Dinosaurs didn’t have a specific diet. Some of them were herbivores and some were meat-eaters. Some of them were as big as a chicken but others were giants – being 50 meters in height is impressive!

The word dinosaur comes from Greek words deinos - terrible and sauros - lizard.

Why and when did dinosaurs disappear?

Dinosaurs first appeared around 240 million years ago. They became the dominant terrestrial animals after the Triassic-Jurassic event 201 million years ago. Dinosaurs went extinct about 65 million years ago, after living on Earth for about 165 million years.


Dinosaurs became extinct because of an asteroid - a large, rocky thing that orbits the Sun. Any fragment of an asteroid that survives landing on Earth becomes known as a meteorite. The asteroid that killed the dinosaurs was around 10 to 15 wide, but it created a much larger crater, 150 kilometres in diameter.
